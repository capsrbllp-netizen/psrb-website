MSME Funding Checklist: What Lenders Actually Look For

By CA Pankaj Sahoo · May 12, 2026 · 5 min read

Most MSME loan rejections aren't about the business idea — they're about the proposal. Banks and NBFCs assess a fixed set of parameters, and missing any one of them slows down or kills an application regardless of how strong the underlying business is.

Documentation Lenders Expect

  • A clear Detailed Project Report (DPR) with realistic assumptions
  • CMA data covering at least 2 years of actuals and 3 years of projections
  • Updated financial statements and ITRs, reconciled with GST returns
  • Collateral or guarantee documentation, where applicable
  • MSME / Udyam registration to access priority-sector benefits

Common Red Flags

Inconsistent figures between the DPR, ITR, and GST returns are the single biggest cause of delay. Lenders cross-check these documents, and discrepancies — even unintentional ones — raise doubts about the reliability of the entire proposal.

How We Help

We prepare bank-ready DPRs and CMA data, structure the proposal around the lender's specific scoring criteria, and directly liaise with banks and NBFCs to negotiate terms — including exploring government schemes like CGTMSE and PMEGP where applicable.
