Pvt Ltd, LLP, or Proprietorship? Choosing the Right Entity for Your Startup

By PSRB Advisory Team · April 15, 2026 · 6 min read

The entity structure you choose on day one of your business shapes your tax outflow, compliance burden, liability exposure, and ability to raise funds — often for years to come. It's a decision worth making deliberately, not by default.

Private Limited Company

Best suited for businesses planning to raise external equity funding. Offers limited liability, a recognised structure for investors, and ESOP flexibility — but comes with higher compliance costs, including statutory audit and ROC filings regardless of size.

Limited Liability Partnership (LLP)

A good middle ground for professional services and small businesses that want limited liability without the compliance overhead of a company. Less attractive to equity investors, but flexible for partner-driven businesses.

Proprietorship / Partnership

Simplest to set up and run, with minimal compliance — but no separation between business and personal liability. Works for very early-stage or low-risk businesses, but should be reassessed as the business scales.

Our Recommendation

We assess this decision against your fundraising plans, risk profile, and expected scale — not just current compliance cost — so the structure you choose supports where the business is going, not just where it is today.
