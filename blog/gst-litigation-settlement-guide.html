Navigating GST Litigation: A Practical Settlement Guide for Businesses

By CA Saubhagya Ranjan Bal · June 10, 2026 · 6 min read

GST litigation has become one of the most common compliance risks faced by businesses in India today. Notices under Section 73/74, mismatches in GSTR-2A/2B reconciliation, and ITC disputes can quickly escalate into prolonged proceedings if not handled with the right strategy from the outset.

Why Early Response Matters

Most adverse GST orders are not the result of a weak case — they are the result of a poorly drafted or delayed response. Tax authorities work on strict timelines, and a reply that doesn't directly address the specific allegation in the notice is treated as deficient, regardless of the underlying merits.

  • Read the notice carefully to identify the exact section and allegation
  • Reconcile books against GSTR-1, GSTR-3B, and GSTR-2A/2B before drafting a reply
  • Maintain a clear paper trail of all supporting documents — invoices, e-way bills, payment proofs
  • Never let a reply deadline lapse without at least a request for extension

When to Consider Settlement

Not every dispute should go to appeal. Where the demand is genuinely defensible on facts, litigation through appellate channels is the right path. But where there is a technical lapse with limited revenue impact, a negotiated settlement — including using amnesty schemes when available — often saves both time and cost.
