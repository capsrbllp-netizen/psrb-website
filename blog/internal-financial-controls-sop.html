Why Your SOPs Aren't Working: Building IFC That Survives an Audit

By CA Laxmi Prasad Maharana · April 30, 2026 · 5 min read

Many businesses have SOP documents sitting in a folder that nobody on the shop floor actually follows. The gap between a written control and a working control is where most audit findings come from — and where most financial leakage hides.

Why Policy Documents Fail

SOPs designed purely from a compliance checklist, without input from the people who actually run the process, rarely survive contact with daily operations. They get bypassed the first time they slow someone down.

What Makes a Control Actually Work

  • Designed with input from operational teams, not just finance
  • Built around existing workflows rather than imposing new ones wholesale
  • Embedded into the ERP/MIS so compliance is the default, not an extra step
  • Reviewed periodically against actual transaction data, not just on paper

Our Approach

With hands-on manufacturing industry experience, we design IFC frameworks and SOPs from the ground up — mapping process risk first, then building controls that are both audit-ready and practical enough to actually be followed.
