Statutory Audit Season: A Readiness Checklist for Companies

By CA Saubhagya Ranjan Bal · March 22, 2026 · 5 min read

A smooth statutory audit doesn't begin when the auditor walks in — it begins months earlier, with how cleanly the books have been maintained through the year. Here's what we ask clients to have in order before fieldwork starts.

Before the Auditor Arrives

  • Bank reconciliations completed monthly, not just at year-end
  • Fixed asset register updated with additions, disposals, and depreciation as per Schedule II
  • Related party transactions identified and documented as they occur
  • Statutory dues (TDS, GST, PF, ESI) reconciled and payment proofs available
  • Board minutes and resolutions for the year filed and accessible

Common Delays We See

The single biggest cause of audit delay is incomplete supporting documentation for large or unusual transactions — discovered only when the auditor asks for it during fieldwork. Keeping a running file through the year, rather than reconstructing it after, saves weeks at audit time.

How We Help

Our statutory audit engagements are partner-led from planning through to sign-off, with a pre-audit readiness review that flags gaps early — so fieldwork stays on schedule and the final report is delivered without last-minute surprises.
